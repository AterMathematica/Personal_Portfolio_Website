Semper Curiosus

My love of learning has guided my decisions since I was young. As far back as I can remember, I've been curious about the world and how it works. With curiosity as a motivator I began accelerating my education by completing high school classes online, and by my junior year, I was a full-time college student.

My love of math and science made the decision to pursue a degree in engineering obvious. In May 2020 I graduated from Lewis-Clark State College (LCSC) with an Associate of Science in Engineering; the same semester I graduated high school.

During my engineering degree I fell deeper in love with the endless applications of mathematics and specifically calculus. I also took an introductory computer science class, where I grew passionate about the process of writing code and solving problems. After graduation, I returned to LCSC to dive deeper into these disciplines, and I began pursuing bachelor degrees in computer science and mathematics. I graduated in May 2024.

In the fall of 2025 I began attending classes at the University of Idaho (UI) as a mathematics major with an emphasis in applied computation. I completed two semesters at UI. I am curious to discover what challenges my future holds as I move forward into my career.
